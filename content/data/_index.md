## About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in social psychology at Université Gustave Eiffel, working at the Laboratoire de Psychologie et d’Ergonomie Appliquées (LaPEA).

My research focuses on how people perceive, interpret, and respond to their environment in contexts where control, uncertainty, and social interactions play a central role. More specifically, I study psychological control, empowerment, learned helplessness, and decision-making across different domains such as organizations, mobility, and everyday social interactions.

---

## Research interests

- Psychological control and empowerment  
- Learned helplessness and disengagement  
- Social cognition and intergroup relations  
- Workplace behavior and organizational justice  
- Decision-making under uncertainty and risk  
- Mobility, road interactions, and safety  

---

## Current work

My current research explores how the type of vehicle people use influences their perception of space, power, and interactions with others on the road. Using experimental designs and immersive methods such as virtual reality, I study how social identity, perceived control, and power shape behavior in traffic situations.

More broadly, my work aims to better understand the psychological mechanisms underlying behavior in complex social environments, with applications to well-being, safety, and public policy.

## Teaching & mentoring

Alongside my research, I have taught a wide range of courses in social psychology, work and organizational psychology, research methods, and statistics, at undergraduate and graduate levels. I particularly enjoy teaching methodology and helping students develop analytical and critical thinking skills.

## Beyond academia

Outside academia, I am drawn to natural environments and open landscapes, and enjoy spending time outdoors, often with my dog. I am really interested in dog training, with a focus on behavior, learning, and well-being.

I have a long-standing interest in mobility and performance. Racing is a central part of that, both through sim racing — where I have spent (probably too much) time on platforms like iRacing — and through my research on the psychology of mobility and road interactions.

I also approach gaming from a competitive perspective, with a strong interest in esports and performance in complex environments.

Beyond that, I play bass guitar, which offers a different and more creative counterpoint to my academic work.
